Impacts of Climate Change on Bavarian Forests


Project Focus and Main Objectives

Specification of threshold values for cultivation of tree species facing climate change.

Details

Climate Change causes a radical shift in temperature and in precipitation over time.
In Europe that leads briefly a higher annual average temperature and less annual precipitation.
Every tree species has its optimum for these parameters.

If the most important conditions change over a period, the planning of forest management has to answer it by using the tree species where they have optimum for the next century. This change in the parameters also forces back some tree species and forest managers and property owners have to know which species should not be planned for the next decades.

The project aims to explore the conditions of the six most important tree species in Bavaria at their dry-warm marginal occurences in South-East Europe. In this way we can analyse how these species produce and live under conditions that will exist in Bavaria in the near future.

My Contribution

Under my supervisor's guidance I was responsible for setting up the best sampling methods and choosing the right parameters to measure in the field. Also I needed to evaluate and visualize the dataset using ArcGIS and statistical methods.
